Overview

The Capital & Cultural Heart

East Sikkim is the most populous and economically developed district of Sikkim, home to the state capital Gangtok. Perched on a ridge at 1,650 m, Gangtok is the nerve centre of governance, commerce, and tourism for the entire state.

The district shares its eastern border with Tibet (China), making it historically and strategically significant. The ancient Silk Route — now a major tourist trail — passes through East Sikkim, connecting India to Tibet via the Nathu La pass at 4,310 m.

East Sikkim hosts the most visited monasteries in the state, including Rumtek — the seat of the Kagyu school of Tibetan Buddhism — and Enchey Monastery, an iconic landmark of Gangtok. The district blends monastic heritage with a lively modern town culture.

Tsomgo Lake (Changu Lake), the ancient Baba Harbhajan Singh Temple, and the views from Tashi Viewpoint make East Sikkim one of the most diverse and rewarding districts for travellers of all kinds.

Sacred Sites

Monasteries & Buddhist Heritage

East Sikkim's monasteries are among the most visited and historically significant in the Himalayan region.

🏯
Rumtek · Kagyu

Rumtek Monastery

The largest monastery in Sikkim and the seat-in-exile of the Kagyu school of Tibetan Buddhism. Built by the 16th Karmapa in the 1960s, Rumtek houses rare Buddhist artefacts, golden chortens, and a vast monastic complex. It is considered one of the most important Kagyu centres in the world.

🌅
Gangtok · Nyingma

Enchey Monastery

Perched at 6,000 ft above Gangtok, Enchey was built in 1909 on a site blessed by Lama Druptob Karpo. The monastery is famous for its annual Chaam dance held in winter, and for its sweeping views over Gangtok and the Kangchenjunga range.

📚
Gangtok · Institute

Namgyal Institute of Tibetology

A world-class research institute and museum dedicated to Tibetan and Buddhist studies. Its collection of rare manuscripts, thangkas, ritual objects, and Lepcha texts is one of the finest in Asia. Essential for anyone interested in Himalayan culture and religion.

🕉️
Gangtok · Temple

Thakurbari Temple

One of the most important Hindu temples in Gangtok, dedicated to Lord Shiva. Surrounded by lush gardens, it serves as a spiritual anchor for the Hindu community in East Sikkim and hosts major festivals including Shivratri.

🌸
Lingdum · Nyingma

Lingdum Monastery

A modern but beautifully designed monastery near Rumtek, built by the Zurmang Kagyud tradition. The monastery complex includes a large prayer hall, meditation retreat quarters, and is set against a forested hillside.

🎖️
Nathu La · Military Shrine

Baba Harbhajan Singh Temple

A unique shrine dedicated to a martyred Indian Army soldier, believed by both Indian and Chinese troops to guard the Nathu La pass. Visited by thousands of pilgrims and soldiers annually; one of the most unusual sacred sites in Sikkim.

Attractions & Landmarks

From ancient passes to glacial lakes — East Sikkim has something for every traveller.

🏔️
Nathu La Pass

A historic mountain pass at 4,310 m on the old Silk Route, now a trade and tourism corridor between India and China. Accessible by permit from Gangtok.

🌊
Tsomgo (Changu) Lake

A glacial lake at 12,310 ft, sacred to the Sikkimese people. The lake changes colour with the seasons and is surrounded by snow-covered mountains and alpine flora.

🌇
MG Marg, Gangtok

The heart of Gangtok — a pedestrian promenade lined with restaurants, shops, and colonial-era buildings. The best place to experience Sikkim's cosmopolitan side.

🌄
Tashi Viewpoint

One of the best panoramic viewpoints in Gangtok, offering clear views of Kangchenjunga and the surrounding Himalayan peaks on clear days.

🌺
Flower Exhibition Centre

A stunning showcase of Sikkim's diverse flora — over 600 species of orchids and other high-altitude flowers displayed seasonally. A must-visit in spring.

🛤️
Old Silk Route

A historic trail through Zuluk, Lungthung, and Nathang Valley — one of Sikkim's most breathtaking road journeys, passing ancient trading posts and offering stunning sunrise views.

Culture & Traditions

Gangtok is Sikkim's cultural melting pot — where Bhutia, Lepcha, and Nepali traditions converge.

🎭

Losar Festival

Tibetan New Year celebrated with masked Chaam dances at Rumtek and Enchey monasteries, drawing pilgrims and tourists from across the world.

🌸

Saga Dawa

The holiest month in the Buddhist calendar — celebrated with processions, prayers, and charitable acts throughout Gangtok and the district's monasteries.

🎨

Thangka Painting

East Sikkim has active workshops producing traditional Tibetan thangka paintings — scroll paintings on cotton or silk depicting Buddhist deities and mandalas.

🍜

Momos & Thukpa

Gangtok's food culture is famous for its Tibetan-influenced cuisine — steamed dumplings (momos), hearty noodle soups (thukpa), and butter tea are staples of daily life.

🎪

Gangtok Food Street

A lively evening market along MG Marg showcasing the culinary diversity of Sikkim — from local fermented foods to Tibetan snacks and Sikkimese craft beer.

Travel Information

East Sikkim is the most well-connected district — the ideal base for exploring the entire state.

✈️ How to Get There

Pakyong Airport (31 km from Gangtok) has flights from Kolkata and Delhi. Nearest railway station is New Jalpaiguri (NJP), 124 km away. Shared jeeps, taxis, and Sikkim Nationalised Transport buses run regularly from NJP to Gangtok.

📄 Permits

  • Indian nationals: Sikkim entry permit at border checkpoints
  • Foreign nationals: Restricted Area Permit (RAP)
  • Nathu La visit: Additional permit required for Indians
  • Foreign nationals cannot visit Nathu La

🌤️ Best Season

Mar–May: Warm, rhododendrons in bloom, best trekking.
Oct–Dec: Crystal clear skies, mountain views, festive season.
Monsoon (Jun–Sep): Lush green but landslide-prone roads.

🏨 Where to Stay

Gangtok offers everything from budget guesthouses to luxury hotels. MG Marg area is most convenient. Book well ahead during peak season (April–May and October–November).
