Overview

The Wilderness of the North

North Sikkim is the largest and most remote district of Sikkim, covering more than half the state's total land area. It is a land of extraordinary natural beauty — towering snow-capped peaks, deep gorges, alpine lakes, and ancient monasteries perched on cliffsides.

The district borders Tibet (China) to the north and east, making it a sensitive frontier zone. Much of the area is a protected high-altitude ecosystem, home to rare flora and fauna including the snow leopard, red panda, and Himalayan blue poppy.

The Lachen and Lachung valleys are the two major inhabited valleys, each with their own distinct communities practising the traditional Dzumsa — a centuries-old system of village governance unique to North Sikkim.

Spiritually, North Sikkim is home to some of Sikkim's most revered Buddhist monasteries, including Gurudongmar Lake — considered one of the holiest lakes in the world — and the Lachen Monastery, a seat of Nyingma Buddhist learning.

Sacred Sites

Monasteries & Holy Sites

North Sikkim's monasteries are among the oldest and most spiritually significant in the entire Himalayan belt.

🏔️
Lachen Valley · Nyingma

Lachen Monastery

One of the most important Buddhist monasteries in North Sikkim, Lachen Monastery is administered by the local Dzumsa council. The monastery houses ancient thangkas, religious texts, and is the spiritual centre of the Lachen community.

❄️
Lachung Valley · Sacred Lake

Gurudongmar Lake

At 17,800 ft, one of the highest lakes in the world. Sacred to Buddhists, Sikhs, and Hindus alike. Legend holds that Guru Padmasambhava blessed the lake, and a small portion never freezes even in the depths of winter.

🙏
Lachung · Nyingma

Lachung Monastery

Perched above the Lachung valley, this 19th-century monastery is decorated with vivid murals depicting Buddhist cosmology. It hosts the annual Chaam masked dance festival each December, drawing pilgrims from across Sikkim.

🌿
Mangan · Ancient Site

Phodong Monastery

Located near the North-East boundary, Phodong is a Kagyu school monastery built in the early 18th century. It is one of the six major monasteries of Sikkim and is known for its annual masked Chaam dance performed before Losar.

⛩️
Chungthang · Confluence

Chungthang Monastery

Situated at the confluence of the Lachen Chu and Lachung Chu rivers, this monastery marks the site where Guru Rinpoche is said to have meditated. The surrounding landscape is considered highly auspicious.

🌊
Thangu · Sacred Lake

Tso Lhamo Lake

At 18,380 ft, possibly the highest lake in India. A sacred site revered by Tibetan Buddhists and considered the source of the Teesta River. Accessible only with special permits, its turquoise waters surrounded by barren highlands leave visitors awestruck.

Natural Attractions

From rhododendron forests to glacial valleys, North Sikkim is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

🌸
Yumthang Valley

Known as the "Valley of Flowers", Yumthang bursts into colour each spring with hundreds of rhododendron species. The Shingba Rhododendron Sanctuary here is a biodiversity hotspot.

🧊
Zero Point (Yumesamdong)

At 15,000 ft, this is the last point on the Lachung route before the Chinese border. Snow remains year-round, offering a surreal arctic landscape amid the Himalayas.

🏕️
Thangu & Chopta Valley

A high-altitude plateau at 13,000 ft used as a summer grazing ground. The Chopta Valley beyond it offers some of the most dramatic mountain panoramas in Sikkim.

🦅
Kangchenjunga Views

North Sikkim offers the closest and most awe-inspiring views of Kangchenjunga — the world's third-highest peak at 8,586 m — from multiple vantage points along the Lachen route.

🌡️
Yumthang Hot Springs

Natural hot springs at the entry of Yumthang Valley, rich in sulphur and believed to have medicinal properties. A perfect respite after a cold mountain journey.

🌿
Shingba Rhododendron Sanctuary

A protected sanctuary spanning 43 sq km, housing over 24 species of rhododendron. The sanctuary is at its most spectacular between April and May when the entire hillside turns crimson and pink.

Living Heritage

Culture & Traditions

North Sikkim's communities have preserved ancient traditions that continue to shape daily life.

🥁

Dzumsa System

A unique centuries-old democratic village council unique to the Lachen and Lachung communities — predating modern governance.

🎭

Chaam Dance

Sacred masked dances performed by monks at monasteries during Losar and Saga Dawa, depicting the defeat of evil forces.

🍵

Butter Tea (Po Cha)

A staple of life in North Sikkim — salt, yak butter, and tea churned together. Sustaining in extreme cold, and an emblem of Tibetan-influenced culture.

🧵

Bhutia Weaving

Intricate patterns woven into traditional Kho and Bakhu garments, each design carrying clan and ceremonial significance.

🎋

Lepcha Crafts

Indigenous Lepcha communities craft bamboo and cane items, bows, and distinctive jewellery, preserving skills passed down for generations.

Travel Information

Everything you need to know before visiting North Sikkim.

🚗 How to Get There

Drive from Gangtok via NH-10. Mangan (district HQ) is ~65 km from Gangtok. Lachung and Lachen are a further 2–3 hours by shared jeep. No railway or airport in the district.

📄 Permits Required

  • Inner Line Permit (ILP) — for Indian nationals
  • Protected Area Permit (PAP) — for foreign nationals
  • Both can be arranged through registered travel agents in Gangtok

🌤️ Best Season

Spring (Mar–May): Rhododendrons in bloom, clear skies.
Autumn (Oct–Nov): Best mountain views, dry roads.
Winter: Heavy snowfall, most roads closed above Lachung.

🏨 Where to Stay

Homestays in Lachen and Lachung villages offer the most authentic experience. Basic guesthouses available in Mangan and Chungthang. Book well in advance for peak season.
