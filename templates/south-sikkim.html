Overview

The Religious Hub of Sikkim

South Sikkim is one of the most densely populated and historically significant districts of the state. Its landscapes transition from subtropical forests at lower elevations to temperate zones higher up, giving it rich biodiversity and year-round agricultural productivity.

The district is renowned as a major pilgrimage destination. Namchi, its headquarters, is home to the iconic Char Dham complex and the towering statue of Guru Padmasambhava — one of the largest in the world — drawing visitors from across India and the globe.

South Sikkim has the highest concentration of cardamom cultivation in Sikkim, earning it an important place in the state's agricultural economy. The rolling green hills dotted with small farms and prayer flags create an iconic Sikkimese landscape.

The district is also known for its proximity to the ancient Tashiding Monastery and the sacred site of Ravangla, which commands sweeping views of the Kangchenjunga range.

Sacred Sites

Monasteries & Pilgrimage Sites

South Sikkim's sacred landscape is a blend of ancient Buddhism and Hindu pilgrimage heritage.

🕌
Tashiding · Nyingma

Tashiding Monastery

Built in 1716, Tashiding is considered the most sacred monastery in Sikkim. Set on a conical hill at the confluence of two rivers, it houses the Thongwa Rangdol chorten — sight of which is believed to absolve all sins. The Bhumchu festival held here each year is among Sikkim's most celebrated.

⛩️
Namchi · Pilgrimage

Char Dham, Namchi

A unique complex replicating all four Char Dham pilgrimage sites of India — Badrinath, Dwarka, Puri, and Rameswaram — set on Solophok Hill. Presided over by a massive 87-ft statue of Lord Shiva, it is a major spiritual destination for Hindu pilgrims.

🙏
Ravangla · Nyingma

Buddha Park, Ravangla

Home to a spectacular 130-ft Buddha statue unveiled in 2013. Set against a panoramic backdrop of the Kangchenjunga range, the park is a serene meditation and pilgrimage destination drawing thousands each year.

🏯
Ralang · Kagyu

Ralang Monastery

A significant Kagyu sect monastery near Ravangla, known for its elaborate Kagyed Chaam dance performed over 10 days each winter. The monastery has an old and new building; the new one is decorated in vivid traditional style.

🌄
Namchi · Guru Statue

Samdruptse Hill

Atop this hill stands one of the world's largest statues of Guru Padmasambhava at 135 ft. The hilltop commands 360° views over South Sikkim's valleys and is considered one of the most sacred spots in the region.

🌿
Borong · Thermal Springs

Borong Monastery & Hot Springs

A remote but rewarding destination — the Borong Monastery sits above natural hot springs believed to have healing properties. The surrounding area is known for its pristine forests and the rare sight of rhododendron and orchid species.

Natural & Cultural Attractions

South Sikkim offers a lush, green landscape rich in biodiversity and scenic viewpoints.

🌄
Ravangla

A hill station at 7,000 ft with sweeping views of Kangchenjunga and the surrounding peaks. Known as a sunrise viewpoint and a gateway to multiple monasteries.

🌿
Maenam Wildlife Sanctuary

A 35 sq km sanctuary at 10,300 ft altitude, home to red pandas, leopards, and over 200 bird species. The summit offers a 360° Himalayan panorama.

🍵
Temi Tea Garden

The only tea garden in Sikkim, producing award-winning organic tea since 1969. Scenic terraced gardens with Kangchenjunga as a backdrop — a photographer's delight.

🌸
Tendong Hill

A sacred hill revered by the Lepcha community. A trekking destination offering dense forests, panoramic views, and the ancient Tendong Hill deity temple at the summit.

🎪
Namchi Trade Fair

An annual event attracting traders and visitors from across Sikkim and neighbouring states. A vibrant showcase of local crafts, cuisine, and cultural performances.

🐦
Kitam Bird Sanctuary

A small but rich bird sanctuary near Namchi, harbouring over 200 species including rare Himalayan birds. A must-visit for birdwatching enthusiasts.

Culture & Traditions

South Sikkim is where Buddhist and Hindu traditions coexist in vibrant harmony.

🎭

Bhumchu Festival

Held at Tashiding, water from a sacred vessel predicts the year's fortunes. One of the most spiritually significant festivals in Sikkim.

🌺

Tihar & Dashain

Celebrated with great enthusiasm by the Nepali community — lamp festivals, family reunions, and colourful rangoli mark the festive season.

🌾

Cardamom Harvest

The annual cardamom harvest in September–October is a community event; families come together to pick, process, and celebrate the spice that defines South Sikkim's economy.

🥘

Sikkimese Cuisine

Gundruk, dhido, and sel roti are staples. South Sikkim's Nepali-influenced cuisine is rich in fermented foods, reflecting the high-altitude agricultural heritage.

🎵

Maruni Dance

A traditional Nepali dance performed during Tihar, characterised by graceful movements and elaborate costumes. A living cultural tradition in South Sikkimese villages.

Travel Information

South Sikkim is the most accessible district for pilgrimage tourism.

🚗 How to Get There

Namchi is 78 km from Gangtok via NH-10 and State Highway. Regular buses and shared jeeps operate from Gangtok, Jorethang, and Melli. A ropeway connects Namchi to Solophok Hill.

📄 Permits

No special permit required for Indian nationals to visit South Sikkim. Foreign nationals need a standard Sikkim permit obtained at border checkpoints or online.

🌤️ Best Season

Oct–Dec: Clear skies, ideal for mountain views and pilgrimage.
Mar–May: Blooming rhododendrons and pleasant weather.
Monsoon (Jun–Sep): Landslides possible; check road conditions.

🏨 Where to Stay

Namchi has a range of hotels and guesthouses. Ravangla offers eco-resorts and homestays with spectacular views. Tashiding has basic accommodation near the monastery.
